Andhra tribal students trek 3 km daily through mud to school due to poor road access

Tribal students in an Andhra Pradesh village walk 3 km daily through muddy paths to attend school in Gorzapadu village. The absence of a proper road puts their safety at risk. Villagers are demanding immediate action.

In Short

  • Andhra students walk 3 km through slush daily
  • No road connectivity between villages
  • Villagers demand urgent government action

Every morning, students of a village in Andhra Pradesh’s Parvathipuram Manyam district brave a 3-kilometre trek through slushy, uneven terrain to reach their school.

With no road connecting the two remote settlements, Tabelu Valasa, where the students hail from, and Gorzapadu, where the school is, this daily journey has become both exhausting and dangerous, especially during the monsoon.

The children, mostly from tribal families, navigate slippery, unpaved tracks that flood easily and become nearly impassable during heavy rain. Locals say the lack of road connectivity not only delays students but also puts their safety and health at risk.

advertisement

Villagers have raised the issue multiple times with public representatives and local government officials, urging them to construct a motorable road. However, their requests have gone unanswered.

“How long will our children have to suffer just to get basic education?” one resident asked, echoing the frustration of many in the hamlet.

The demand is simple, residents say: a proper road to ensure safe, uninterrupted access to education. Until then, the tribal students of Tabelu Valasa will continue trudging through mud for the right to learn.

A similar issue was recently reported from Solubongu village in Alluri Seetharamaraju district, where tribal students had to cross a reservoir and walk kilometres to reach school. Days after the matter was reported by India Today, officials swung into action, sanctioning road work and a special school for the village.
